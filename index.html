Port-au-Prince, Haiti

Port-au-Prince is the capital and most populous of Haiti's communes, whose urban area is estimated to have 2,754,812 inhabitants (2019) called Port-au-Princiens and Port-au-Princiennes. Administratively, Port-au-Prince is both the capital of the Department of the West and of the Port-au-Prince district. It is the second largest French-speaking city in America, after Montreal. It took the name of Port-Républicain during the French Revolution.
